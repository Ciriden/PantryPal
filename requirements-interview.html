Requirements Interview

This section contains the notes from the initial requirements interview.

  • PantryPal Software Requirements

Core Features

  • Recipe Creation & Management: Users can create, manage, edit, and delete recipes, with step-by-step instructions, ingredient amounts and units, and prep/cooking timers.
  • Allergen Tracking: Include allergens for each recipe and ingredient, with alerts for users when allergens are present.
  • Shopping Cart & Pantry: Auto-generate shopping carts from recipes, with manual editing options. Items in the cart can be added to the pantry inventory, which dynamically updates as users add/remove ingredients.
  • Recipe Scaling: Users can adjust recipe servings and ingredients accordingly.
  • Ingredient Substitutions: Suggest substitutions based on availability or dietary preferences.
  • Visual Design: Focus on minimalist design with a visual-first approach (more images, less text). Homepage should be simple with a 3-part layout.
  • Accessibility: Include accessibility features to accommodate different user needs.
  • Customizable Photos: Users can upload custom images for recipes and ingredients.
  • User Authentication: Login system with username/password for secure access.
  • Privacy Settings: Users can set privacy preferences for sharing recipes and other content.

Stretch Goals

  • Social Features: Ability to rate, comment, and share recipes with others, along with future exploration of additional social capabilities.

Other Considerations

  • No expiration dates for ingredients in recipes.
  • Tracking of cooking time and task completion within the app.
  • QR codes for ingredient packaging or recipe sharing may be considered.
  • Target Audience: Everyday home users needing an intuitive, easy-to-use solution for recipe and grocery management.

Design & Functionality Notes

  • The application should have a clean, simple, and minimalist design with a strong visual focus. The target audience is home users who need a straightforward tool to manage recipes, ingredients, and shopping lists.
